Basic Definitions

Time: A measure of the sequence and duration of events. In mechanics, it is treated as a continuous scalar quantity.

Distance: The total length of the path traveled, a scalar quantity, measured in meters (m).

Mass: A measure of the amount of matter in an object, also representing its resistance to acceleration when a force is applied. Measured in kilograms (kg).

Vectors Overview

A vector is a mathematical object that has both magnitude and direction. Examples in mechanics include displacement, velocity, acceleration, and force. Vectors are often represented as arrows in diagrams or as components in coordinate systems.

Calculus in Mechanics

Calculus provides the mathematical tools to describe change and motion. Derivatives are used to find quantities like velocity (rate of change of position) and acceleration (rate of change of velocity). Integrals are used to determine quantities like displacement from velocity or work from force.

Newtonâ€™s Three Laws

  1. First Law (Inertia): A body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force.
  2. Second Law: The acceleration of an object is proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. (F = ma)
  3. Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
